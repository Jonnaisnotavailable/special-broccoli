APGAR Scoring

APGARstands for:

A - Appearance (skin color)

P - Pulse (heart rate)

G - Grimace (Response)

A - Activity (muscle tone)

R- Respiration (breathing ability)

APGAR Sign 2 1 0
Appearance (skin color) Normal color all over (hands & feet are pink) Normal color (but hands & feet are bluish) Bluish-gray or pale all over
Pulse (heart rate) Normal (above 100bpm) Below 100 bpm Absent (no pulse)
Grimace ("reflex irritability" Pulls away, sneezes, cough, or cries with stimulation Facial movement only with stimulation Absent (no response to stimulation)
Activity (muscle tone) Active, spontaneous movement Arms & legs flexed with little movement No movement
Respiration (breating rate & effort) Normal rate & effort, good cry Slow or irregular breathing, weak cry Absent (no breathing)

APGAR is created in 1952 by Dr. Virginia Apgar, and used her name as mnemonic for each of the five categories that a person will score. This test checks a baby's heart rate, muscle tone, and other signs to see if extra medical care or emergency care is needed. The test is usually given twice: at 1 minute after birth, and again at 5 minutes after birth.

Purpose:

1. Evaluate the conditions of the baby at birth,

2. Determine the need for resuscitation,

3. Evaluate the effectiveness of resuscitative efforts,

4. To identify neonates at risk for morbidity and mortality.

APGAR SCORE MEAN?

0-3- In danger, needs resuscitation

4-6- Fair condition

7-10- Good condition
